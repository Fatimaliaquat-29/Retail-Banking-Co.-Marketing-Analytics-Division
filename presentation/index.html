PROJECT PRESENTATION

Optimizing Direct Marketing Campaigns

A Machine Learning Approach to Predict Term Deposit Subscriptions

Direct telemarketing is costly and causes customer fatigue. This project details the end-to-end implementation of an ML targeting pipeline that increases conversion rates and reduces campaign operational costs.

The Challenge: Imbalance & Inefficiencies

Direct Telemarketing Inefficiencies

Randomly calling customers from a database is highly inefficient and creates negative customer sentiment. Our goal is to identify high-probability prospects beforehand.

The Data Context

Using the UCI Bank Marketing Dataset (41,188 rows, 21 variables) spanning May 2008 to November 2010.

Severe Class Imbalance: 88.73% said "No" vs 11.27% who said "Yes". Standard accuracy is a trap (a dummy model gets 88.7% accuracy by predicting only "No").
03

Key Insights & Leakage Control

Statistical Profile of a Subscriber

  • Interest Rates Matter: Subscribed clients were contacted when 3-month Euribor rates were low (avg 2.12% vs 3.81% for non-subscribers).
  • Campaign Limits: Non-subscribers averaged 2.63 contacts while subscribers averaged 2.05 contacts. Success falls off after 3-4 calls.
Dropped Housing & Personal Loans: Statistical tests (Chi-Square) confirmed housing and personal loans are not statistically significant (p-values: 0.058 and 0.579).
Critical Leakage Feature: Call Duration

Call length in seconds is highly predictive, but it is only known after a call is placed. At prediction time, duration is zero. Retaining duration in training is a leakage bug. We completely dropped it to build a realistic, deployable model.

04

Modeling Strategy & Imbalance

Pipeline Engineering

A robust ColumnTransformer pipeline handles the features properly:

  • Train-Test Split: 80/20 stratified partition on target.
  • Numerical scale: StandardScaler for normalization.
  • Categorical encoding: OneHotEncoder, keeping "unknown" values as a distinct level to prevent sample size drops.

Resolving the Imbalance

To overcome the 11.27% positive class skew, we designed and tested several methodologies:

  1. Cost-Sensitive Learning: Penalizing positive class errors.
  2. Resampling (ROS, RUS): Modifying training sets.
  3. SMOTE: Creating synthetic minority subscriber examples.
05

Model Leaderboard & Recommended Choice

Why Tuned Random Forest?

We tested 11 models. Tuned Random Forest yielded the best trade-off:

  • CatBoost: High accuracy (90.22%) but poor Recall (23.17%). Misses 76% of subscribers.
  • SMOTE LogReg: High Recall (65.84%) but poor Precision (35.81%). Too many wasted calls.
  • Tuned RF: Best balanced F1-score of 51.27% (captures 61% of subscribers with 44.22% precision).

06

Strategic Action Plan for ROI

1. Deploy Model lead-scoring

Only call customers predicted positive by the model. This lifts list conversion from 11.3% to 44.2% (a 3.9x improvement).

2. Interest Rate-Driven Campaigns

Macro factors represent 46% of model weights. Double campaign volume during low Euribor rate periods when customers seek deposit terms.

3. Operational Threshold Tuning

Shift threshold from 0.5 to 0.4 if call capacity increases. This captures 73.38% of total subscribers with 27.16% precision.

4. Enforce Call Capping

Cap customer contacts to 3-4 per campaign to prevent fatigue and waste.

Exploratory Data Analysis Insights

Target & Category Skewness

Key Dataset Profile

41,188 Total Records
11.27% Subscribed (Yes)
88.73% Unsubscribed (No)

Age Demographics

Average age is 40.02 years. The youngest client was 17, and the oldest was 98. Subscriptions show a U-shape curve, higher among students and elderly retirees.

Contacts Capping Recommendation

The database shows some clients called up to 56 times. Over 42.8% of clients were contacted only once. Success probabilities decrease significantly after 3 attempts.

Loan Feature Non-Significance

Chi-Square tests show Housing Loan (p=0.058) and Personal Loan (p=0.579) have no statistically significant relationship with subscription. Debt status is not an obstacle to saving.

Euribor 3M Interest Rates

Mean rate was 3.62%. Highly bimodal (centered at 1.4% and 4.86%). Subscriptions increase in low Euribor rate conditions (avg 2.12% vs 3.81% for non-subscribers).
